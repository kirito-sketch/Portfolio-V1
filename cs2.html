What we analyzed:
  • Ad-Ops workflows are reactive, not proactive. Users logged in 2.1x/day. Critical mid-day issues went unnoticed.
  • Context switching kills efficiency. Managers toggled between 5 platforms 47 times per session.
  • No prioritization framework. All campaigns presented equally — which needs attention RIGHT NOW?
Delayed anomaly detection Lack of actionable alerts Fragmented workflow Manual ROI calculations Ambiguous status indicators No campaign prioritization

Framework

Core design principles

01

Alert Before It Hurts

Surface anomalies as they happen, not hours later.

02

Context Over Raw Numbers

Every metric needs a comparison point: vs yesterday, vs target, vs threshold.

03

Reduce to Next Action

Don't make users figure out what to do — suggest the action.

04

Visual Health Scoring

Status badges (LOSING, SCALING, PROFITABLE) replace ambiguous states.

05

Eliminate Platform Hopping

Embed controls directly in the interface.

Transformation

Dashboard evolution

Before: Passive Reporting
  • Single metric card, no alerts
  • Generic chart — dips and spikes demand manual investigation
After: Active Monitoring
  • Three hero metrics with real-time deltas
  • Action Center surfaces problems proactively
Transformation

Prioritized campaign management

Campaigns Command

Top-level KPI cards, smart filtering with automatic triage (Critical tab), and campaign cards with visual health scoring — LOSING, SCALING, PROFITABLE — replace the flat table.

LOSING ROI < 1.0x
SCALING ROI > 2.5x
PROFITABLE ROI 1.2x–2.5x
PAUSED Inactive

Reflection

Learnings & future opportunities

What Worked

Co-Design with Power Users

Watching users manually calculate ROI in spreadsheets — while sitting in front of our dashboard — was the moment everything clicked.

Real-Time as a Feature

Users valued the "Updating in real-time" badge as much as the data itself.

What We'd Improve

Predictive Alerts

Next iteration: "At current burn rate, this campaign will hit $50K loss by EOD."

Mobile Command Center

Push notifications for true 24/7 monitoring on the go.

Conclusion

A tool became a teammate
