Project Overview

Outline

This project showcases how we identified critical gaps in the real-time monitoring and proactive management of high-velocity ad campaigns across Facebook, Google, and TikTok. By addressing Ad-Ops managers' inability to detect campaign anomalies before significant budget loss, through intelligent alerts, contextual performance scoring, and embedded action controls, we transformed Statera from a retrospective reporting tool into an active command center that drives measurable impact across key business metrics.

Key Impacts

-47% Reduction in time to identify underperforming campaigns
+3.2x Increase in proactive campaign adjustments

What we analyzed:
  • Ad-Ops workflows are reactive, not proactive. Users logged in 2.1 times per day. Critical mid-day issues went unnoticed.
  • Context switching kills efficiency. Managers toggled between 5 platforms an average of 47 times per session.
  • "Normal" vs "Abnormal" required mental math. Users calculated ROI manually by cross-referencing spend and revenue.
  • Alert fatigue from external tools. One user had 127 unread "campaign update" emails without context.
  • The "In Progress" status created uncertainty. Users couldn't distinguish "processing" vs "stuck for 3 days."
  • No prioritization framework. All campaigns presented equally — which needs attention RIGHT NOW?
Delayed anomaly detection Lack of actionable alerts Fragmented workflow Manual ROI calculations Ambiguous status indicators No campaign prioritization
Platform Limitation

Statera's data pipeline operated on a 4-hour batch processing cycle, pulling metrics from Facebook, Google, and TikTok APIs at scheduled intervals. For high-velocity campaigns — especially mobile app installs running $500+/hour budgets — this architecture meant:

  • A campaign could hemorrhage $2,000 in 4 hours before appearing in the dashboard
  • ROI calculations reflected 6–8 hour old data, making optimization decisions based on stale information
  • Campaign status updates (paused, active, completed) had similar delays
  • All analysis happened client-side — users manually filtered, segmented, and interpreted data with no intelligence layer

This wasn't a design problem. This was an architectural constraint that made real-time decision-making impossible.

Framework

Core design principles

01

Alert Before It Hurts

Surface anomalies as they happen, not hours later.

02

Context Over Raw Numbers

Every metric needs a comparison point: vs yesterday, vs target, vs threshold.

03

Reduce to Next Action

Don't make users figure out what to do — suggest the action.

04

Visual Health Scoring

Status badges (LOSING, SCALING, PROFITABLE) replace ambiguous states.

05

Eliminate Platform Hopping

Embed controls directly in the interface.

Reflection

Learnings & future opportunities

What Worked

Co-Design with Power Users

Embedding with Ad-Ops teams during active campaigns gave us insights no interview could capture. Watching a user manually calculate ROI in a spreadsheet while sitting in front of our dashboard was the "aha moment."

Solving for "Unknown Unknowns"

The Action Center doesn't just answer questions users ask — it surfaces problems they didn't know to look for.

Real-Time as a Feature

Users valued the "Updating in real-time" badge as much as the data itself — it restored confidence that they weren't making decisions on stale information.

What We'd Improve

Predictive Alerts

Current alerts are reactive ("ROI is 0.42x"). Next iteration: predictive alerts ("At current burn rate, this campaign will hit $50K loss by EOD").

Multi-User Collaboration

Adding "Campaign assigned to [User]" or "Alert acknowledged by [User]" would prevent duplicate work across distributed teams.

Mobile Command Center

A mobile companion app with push notifications would enable true 24/7 monitoring for on-the-go Ad-Ops managers.
